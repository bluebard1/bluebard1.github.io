Free Space Wave Propagation: Diffraction Simulator using Angular Spectrum Method (ASM)
Scalar diffraction represents a common approach to model the interaction of monochromatic electromagnetic waves with any given aperture.

The accepted practice in the field of wave optics is to model this propagation (wave movement after aperture) as an optical component not unlike other optical components such as lenses or mirrors. Beam propagation is visualized by solving the Fresnel approximation with ASM and plotting the resulting field.

Simulating Electromagnetic Field Distribution using Finite Elements Method (FEM) in Python
FEM represents a robust approach to solve differential equations numerically. It is widely used in mechanical and electrical engineering to compute electric and magnetic field distributions, or tension and force exposure of mechanical components.

The Python routine uses Netgen/NGSolve to analyse and visualize the electric field between two electrodes and Foucault's currents in a ferromagnetic core surrounded by a copper coil.
